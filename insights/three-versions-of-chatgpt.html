There Are Three Versions of ChatGPT. Your Marketing Team Is Optimizing for One.

By Alicia Childers April 29, 2026 4 min read

A marketer I know asked ChatGPT for the weather and got an answer that was simply wrong. Not because the tool is broken, but because they were talking to a version of ChatGPT that was answering from memory instead of looking at live information. That small moment captures a much bigger problem for anyone trying to get found in AI.

Most teams treat ChatGPT as one thing. It is not. The answer a person receives depends heavily on which version they are using, and each one pulls from a different place. If your marketing only accounts for one of them, you are invisible in the others.

The three versions, and why they answer differently

1. The model answering from training. This is ChatGPT working from what it already learned. It is fast and confident, but it does not know about anything recent, and it cannot see your latest page. If your business is not part of what the model absorbed, you do not exist in this answer.

2. The model that searches the live web. This version goes and looks. It pulls current pages, cites sources, and can surface a business it has never been trained on, if that business is readable and trustworthy. This is where fresh, well-structured content wins.

3. The model living inside other products. ChatGPT is also embedded in tools, assistants, and workflows through its API. The behavior there depends on how the product is built and what data it is allowed to reach. Your customer may be using ChatGPT without ever opening ChatGPT.

Why this matters for visibility

Optimizing for only one version is like buying a billboard on a road your customers stopped driving on. You can be perfectly visible in the trained model and completely absent from the live-search version, or the other way around. The weather mistake was harmless. The same gap, applied to "who is the best provider near me," sends a real customer to your competitor.

What to do about it

You cannot control which version someone opens, so you have to earn a presence across all three. That means content the trained models will absorb over time, current and structured pages the live-search version can read today, and a clean, machine-readable footprint that holds up wherever your business gets pulled into an answer. It is the same foundation, working in three places at once.

The takeaway for your business: Stop asking "does ChatGPT know us" as if there is one answer. Ask whether you show up in the trained model, the live-search model, and the embedded model. The businesses that cover all three are the ones AI recommends no matter how the customer arrives.
